Exercise: Counting Pairs with Even Sum Problem Statement You are given two sets of items. The first set contains N items of one type, and the second set contains M items of another type. Your task is to find the number of ways to choose two items such that a certain condition (based on their types) holds. The condition is that the sum of their characteristics must meet a specific requirement. The items can be selected in any order. Requirements Count how many pairs of items can be selected such that the sum of their characteristics meets the condition. The sum is considered valid if both items share the same characteristic. Constraints 0 <= N, M <= 100 2 <= N + M All inputs are integers.
