**Exercise Title: Calculate the Circumference of a Circle**

**Problem Statement:**  
Create a program that calculates the circumference of a circle given its radius. The program should use the formula for the circumference of a circle and print the result.

**Requirements:**

1. **Input:**  
   - The program should read an integer from the user, which represents the radius of a circle. You can assume the input will always be a positive integer.

2. **Processing:**  
   - Use the formula for the circumference of a circle:  
     \(C = 2 \times \pi \times R\)  
     where \(C\) is the circumference and \(R\) is the radius of the circle.  
   - Use an appropriate value for \(\pi\), maintaining a precision that is suitable for the calculation of the circumference.

3. **Output:**  
   - Print the resulting circumference of the circle. Ensure the printed value is either a float or double to maintain precision.

4. **Constraints:**  
   - The radius \(R\) will be a positive integer, and its value will be constrained within reasonable limits to ensure calculations remain precise.  
   - Your program's output should be precise, allowing for a small margin of absolute or relative error, not exceeding 0.01.

By completing this exercise, you will practice working with basic arithmetic operations, understanding of how to use constants in computations, and formatting numeric output for precision.
