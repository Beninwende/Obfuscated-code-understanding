**Exercise Title: Calculate the Distance Between Two Moving Points**

**Problem Statement:**

In this exercise, you will calculate the distance between two moving points. Imagine two rods, each with a length attached at one end to the same origin point (0, 0) on a 2D Cartesian plane. These rods rotate at different speeds around this origin, forming angles with the positive x-axis. The first rod completes one full rotation in `T1` minutes, and the second rod completes one in `T2` minutes.

At a given time instant specified by `t` minutes, calculate the distance between the free endpoints of these two rods (i.e., the distance from the end of the first rod to the end of the second rod).

**Requirements:**

1. Implement a function that takes input values:
   - `L1`: the length of the first rod (an integer).
   - `L2`: the length of the second rod (an integer).
   - `T1`: the time in minutes it takes for the first rod to complete one full rotation (an integer).
   - `T2`: the time in minutes it takes for the second rod to complete one full rotation (an integer).
   - `t`: the time in minutes at which the distance should be calculated (an integer).

2. Output the distance between the endpoints of the two rods (a floating-point number).

3. Ensure that the output is accurate to at least 10 decimal places. 

4. Assume all measurements are in minutes and centimeters, and that the functions of the angles of rotation are continuous and proportional.

By the end of this exercise, you should be able to apply trigonometric functions to compute the distance between two points based on their respective angular velocities and time-based positioning on a rotational path.
