## Exercise Title: Maximum Sum Using Two Numbers

### Problem Statement
You are given two integers, `X` and `Y`. Your task is to select two numbers, either from `X`, `Y`, or a combination of both, such that the sum of these two numbers is maximized. You can pick the same number twice or choose one number from each.

Each selection reduces the available number by one for future selections. For example, if you choose `X` first, your second choice should be `X-1` or `Y`.

Your goal is to determine the maximum possible sum of the two selected numbers.

### Requirements
1. Implement a function that accepts two integer parameters, `X` and `Y`.
2. The function should compute and return the largest possible sum from selecting two numbers according to the rules stated.
3. Ensure your function handles all edge cases within the given constraints:
   - Both `X` and `Y` are non-negative integers.
   - `X` and `Y` are at least 3 and at most 20.
