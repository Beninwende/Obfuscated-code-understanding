Exercise: Circle Inside Rectangle Problem Statement: Write a program that determines whether a circle is completely inside a rectangle. You are given the dimensions of the rectangle and the circle's position and radius. The rectangle is defined by its width (W) and height (H), with the top-right corner located at (W, H). The circle is defined by its center at the point (x, y) and its radius r. Your task is to check if the entire circle fits inside the rectangle. A circle fits inside a rectangle if all parts of the circle are within the rectangle’s boundaries, meaning that: The leftmost point of the circle (x - r) should be greater than or equal to 0. The rightmost point of the circle (x + r) should be less than or equal to W. The topmost point of the circle (y - r) should be greater than or equal to 0. The bottommost point of the circle (y + r) should be less than or equal to H. Requirements: Implement the logic to read the values for W, H, x, y, and r. Check if the circle is completely contained within the rectangle using the above conditions. Print 'Yes' if the circle is inside the rectangle, otherwise print 'No'. Make sure to handle the constraints properly and implement basic error checking as needed.
