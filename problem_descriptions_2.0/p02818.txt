**Exercise Title: Track Cookie Consumption**

**Problem Statement:**
In this exercise, you will simulate a cookie-eating sequence involving two individuals. Let's call them Person A and Person B. Both have a certain number of cookies to begin with. The task is to model how the cookies are consumed over a specified number of actions. The eating sequence follows a specific order:

1. If Person A has at least one cookie, they eat one.
2. If Person A has no cookies but Person B has at least one, Person B eats one.
3. If neither has cookies, no action is taken.

Your goal is to determine how many cookies each person has remaining after all actions have been executed.

**Requirements:**

1. Write a function to input three parameters: 
   - `cookiesA` (the initial number of cookies Person A has).
   - `cookiesB` (the initial number of cookies Person B has).
   - `actions` (the number of cookie-eating actions to perform).

2. The function should simulate the action sequence detailed above for a given number of steps.

3. After performing all actions, the function should return the number of cookies remaining for Person A and Person B as a tuple or similar data structure that your chosen programming language supports.

4. Make use of conditionals and loops to simulate the process efficiently, taking into account that the number of cookies and actions can be large.

5. Your solution should handle edge cases, such as when either person starts with zero cookies or when the number of actions exceeds the total number of cookies available.

The exercise aims to strengthen understanding of basic control flow, large number handling, and function design, all fundamental skills in programming.
