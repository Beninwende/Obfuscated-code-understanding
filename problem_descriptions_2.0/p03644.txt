**Exercise Title: Find the Largest Power of 2**

**Problem Statement:**

Your task is to find a specific number based on its divisibility by 2. Given a positive integer `N`, you need to determine the largest power of 2 that is less than or equal to `N`. The number must be divisible by 2 for the maximum number of times among all integers from 1 to `N` (inclusive).

The property of divisibility by 2 that you should consider is the number of times an integer can be divided by 2 without leaving a remainder. 

For example, if you are examining the number 8, it can be repeatedly divided by 2 to yield 4, then 2, and finally 1. This process indicates that 8 is divisible by 2 three times.

Your solution should involve simple calculations to determine this number efficiently.

**Requirements:**

1. Function should accept a single integer input, `N`.
2. The output should be the largest power of 2 (≤ N) that can be divided by 2 the maximum number of times.
3. Ensure that your solution runs efficiently, even for large values within the given constraints.
4. Your program must handle all values of `N` according to the specified constraints.

**Constraints:**

- 1 ≤ N ≤ 100

Implement your solution based on these specifications. Your code should efficiently compute the correct number and adhere to provided constraints to ensure accuracy and optimality.
