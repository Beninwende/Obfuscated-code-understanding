Exercise: Maximum Operations on a Sequence Problem Statement You are given a sequence of integers, and you can perform two types of operations on each element of the sequence: Divide by 2: If the element is divisible by 2, you can divide it by 2. Multiply by 3: You can multiply the element by 3. However, you cannot apply the Multiply by 3 operation to every element at once, and the value of each element after an operation must remain an integer. Your goal is to determine the maximum number of times you can perform these operations on the sequence until no further operations can be applied. Requirements You are given an integer N representing the length of the sequence. You are also given a sequence of N integers: a_1, a_2, a_3, ..., a_N. You must return the maximum number of operations that can be performed on the entire sequence. Concepts Covered Looping through sequences. Integer arithmetic (dividing by 2 and multiplying by 3). Handling constraints on operation validity (integers must remain valid after each operation). Goal The problem focuses on counting the maximum number of valid operations that can be applied across the sequence, utilizing both types of operations as much as possible.
