**Title: Find Maximum Widgets Produced**

**Problem Statement:**

You are tasked with determining the optimal production strategy for a factory that produces different types of widgets. The factory utilizes a limited stock of material to produce these widgets. Given several types of widgets, each requiring a specific amount of material, your goal is to find the maximum number of widgets that can be produced while ensuring at least one of each type is made.

Here's the detailed situation:

- The factory produces `T` different kinds of widgets: Widget 1, Widget 2, ..., Widget T.
- For each Widget `i` (1 ≤ `i` ≤ T), a certain number of units of material `u_i` is required to produce one widget.
- The factory has a supply of `M` units of material available.
- The factory policy demands that at least one widget of each type must be produced.

Your task is to compute the maximum number of widgets that can be produced, complying with the factory policy. It is not mandatory to use all the material available.

**Requirements:**

1. Determine the total number of widgets that can be produced given the constraints.
2. Ensure that at least one of every type of widget is manufactured.
3. Use the available material efficiently without violating the constraints mentioned.

**Constraints:**

- The number of widget types `T` is such that 2 ≤ `T` ≤ 100.
- The material units required `u_i` for each widget type satisfies 1 ≤ `u_i` ≤ 1000.
- The sum of material units `u_1 + u_2 + ... + u_T` required to produce one widget of each type is less than or equal to the total material available `M` (i.e., `u_1 + u_2 + ... + u_T ≤ M ≤ 10^5`).
- All inputs are integers.
