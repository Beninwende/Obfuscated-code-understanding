# Exercise: Factor Pair Digit Counting

## Problem Statement

You are given a positive integer `X`. Your task is to identify two positive integers, `P` and `Q`, such that `X = P × Q`. For these integers, define the function `G(P, Q)` as the larger of the following: the number of digits in the decimal notation of `P`, and the number of digits in the decimal notation of `Q`. Your goal is to determine the minimum value of `G(P, Q)` among all possible pairs `(P, Q)` that satisfy the given equation.

## Requirements

1. **Input**:
   - A single positive integer `X`.

2. **Output**:
   - Print the minimum value of `G(P, Q)`.

3. **Constraints**:
   - Consider all possible positive integer pairs `(P, Q)` such that `P × Q = X`.
